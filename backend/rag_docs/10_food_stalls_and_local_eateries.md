Popular Food Spots Near Main Mandals
The Ganeshotsav pilgrimage is also a culinary one, with many of Pune's most historic eateries located in the Peth areas, having served devotees and traders for over a century.

Near Tulshibaug ('Manacha Chautha') & Mandai Market:

Shri Krishna Bhuvan

Location: By-lanes of Tulshibaug, near Moti Chowk.   

History: A legendary eatery founded in 1941 by Yeshwant Joshi. It originally opened at 4:30 AM to serve farmers and traders visiting the nearby Mahatma Phule Mandai.   

Must-Try: Their unique Misal, which is made with a base of potato bhaaji and poha (not traditional sprouts) and is famously served with sliced bread instead of pav.   

Near Dagdusheth Ganpati (Budhwar/Sadashiv/Shaniwar Peth):

Vaidya Uphar Gruha

Location: Budhwar Peth.   

History: One of Pune's oldest and most revered misal joints, established in 1910.   

Must-Try: Famous for its distinct 'green misal' (less spicy).   

Sudamache Pohe

Location: Dagdusheth Temple Road, opposite the Shaniwar Wada main gate.   

Must-Try: A classic and quick stop for 'pohe' (flattened rice), perfectly located between landmarks.   

Other Famous Misal:

Poona Misal House: Located in Sadashiv Peth.   

Siddheshwari Misal: Located in Shaniwar Peth, known for Nashik-style 'kala masala' misal.   

Darpan Misal: Located in Raviwar Peth, a hidden gem popular with locals.   

Vada Pav:

Joshi Wadewale: A classic and highly-rated Pune chain with outlets in the area.   

Garden Vada Pav Centre: Another city-wide icon known for its taste.
