Profiles: Top Mandals & Practical Notes (Pune 2025)
This document lists Pune's 'Manache Ganpati' (The Honored Five) and other significant mandals, providing historical context and visitor notes.

The 'Manache Ganpati' (The Honored Five) This is the official hierarchy established by Lokmanya Tilak, defining the order of precedence for the final immersion procession.   

Shree Kasba Ganpati ('Manacha Pahila')

Honor: First Honored (Gram Devta of Pune).   

History: Commissioned by Jijabai Bhosale, mother of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, around the 1630s. The idol is 'Swayambhu' (self-originated).   

Notes: Leads the Visarjan procession. Located in Kasba Peth, the oldest part of the city.   

Tambdi Jogeshwari Ganpati ('Manacha Dusra')

Honor: Second Honored (Gram Devi of Pune).   

History: Housed in the 15th-century temple of Goddess Durga (Jogeshwari Devi). The Peshwas reportedly sought blessings here before military missions.   

Notes: The Ganpati idol is unique, resembling an African elephant. It is displayed in a silver dome  and is immersed and re-created (a new replica) every year.   

Guruji Talim Ganpati ('Manacha Tisra')

Honor: Third Honored.   

History: Established in 1887, before Tilak's 1893 mass movement.   

Notes: A powerful symbol of Hindu-Muslim unity, it was co-founded by a Hindu family (Bhiku Shinde) and a Muslim family (Ustaad Nalban).   

Tulshibaug Ganpati ('Manacha Chautha')

Honor: Fourth Honored.   

History: Established in 1901.   

Notes: Famous for its massive 15-foot idol, the tallest in Pune. The idol has been made of fiberglass since 1975/1978  and is adorned with over 80kg of gold and silver. Renowned for its elaborate, moving mythological tableaus (decorations) since 1952, traditionally managed by the Khatavkar family.   

Kesari Wada Ganpati ('Manacha Pachva')

Honor: Fifth Honored.   

History: Established by Lokmanya Tilak himself in 1893.   

Notes: Located in Kesari Wada, which was Tilak's home and the office for his revolutionary newspaper, 'Kesari'. This mandal cements Tilak's own legacy within the festival structure he popularized.   

Other Significant Mandals

Shrimant Dagdusheth Halwai Ganapati

Notes: Pune's most popular and wealthiest mandal. Known for its opulent gold-adorned idol  and extensive philanthropic work. (See 01_dagdusheth.md for full details).   

Shrimant Bhausaheb Rangari Ganpati

History: Established in 1892, a year before Tilak's movement. It claims to be the first-ever Sarvajanik Ganpati.   

Founder: Shrimant Bhausaheb Rangari (Bhausaheb Lakshman Jawle), a 'Rajvaidya' (royal physician) and freedom fighter.   

Idol: The idol is unchanged since 1892, made of wood and bran. It is unique and fiercely political, depicting Ganesha slaying a demon that explicitly symbolized British colonial rule.   

Akhil Mandai Mandal (Mandai Ganpati)

History: Established by the vegetable vendors of the Mahatma Jyotiba Phule market.   

Idol: Famous for its iconic 'Sharada-Ganesh' idol (depicting Ganesha alongside Goddess Sharada).
